About Us

Click above to see more information aboutsome of the past projects completed and events held in the areas of criminal justice, health, education, political action, international affairs, and environmental justice.

We are the Stanford chapter of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP). Initially founded by several members of the black community in response to both issues on campus and in the surrounding community. We have expanded both in size and mission to fight for the equality of all peoples against all forms of discrimination and oppression, not just limited to race-based discrimination.

Gallery

TGIJP [Criminal Justice]

Since 2013, we have been cultivating a relationship with the Transgender Gender-Variant Justice Project, an organization that works to support transgender women inside and outside of prison.

From Classrooms to Cells: Unraveling the School-to-Prison Pipeline [Education]

Our work has included and involvement in the "Who's Teaching Us" campaign, devoted to raising awareness surrounding faculty diversity (or the lack thereof) here at Stanford.

The Not So Sunny Side of Stanford: A Discussion on the Duck Syndrome in the Black Community [Health]

In winter quarter of 2015, the Health Committee hosted a panel event that allowed four students from Stanford's Black community to share their struggles and breakthroughs with mental health and the duck syndrome to a group of about thirty members from the Stanford community.
